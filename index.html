About

My path into biomedical engineering has been shaped by a consistent goal: using engineering design to move health-focused technologies closer to real-world impact.

I began with a foundation in chemical engineering and pre-medical studies, where I learned to think across materials, transport, chemistry, biology, and human health. That background taught me how engineered systems behave at both the physical and biological level, and it shaped my interest in technologies that can help us better understand and treat disease.

My early research in orthopedic biomechanics and biomaterials connected that foundation to tissue regeneration. Working with 3D-printable biopolymer formulations for bone scaffold applications introduced me to the challenges of designing materials that are not only mechanically functional, but biologically meaningful.

At Sandia National Laboratories, I expanded that perspective through rapid prototyping, additive manufacturing, and device-focused research and development. Designing and testing components taught me how engineering ideas move from concept to physical system, and how manufacturability, material behavior, and design iteration shape the development of functional technologies.

Today, my Ph.D. work in Dr. Corey Neu’s lab at the University of Colorado Boulder brings these experiences together. I develop, characterize, and computationally model joint-on-chip medical device platforms for osteoarthritis research, regenerative therapy development, and drug discovery. My work bridges mechanical design, biomaterials, microfluidics, mechanobiology, and translational health by creating tools that help researchers study disease processes in more controlled and physiologically relevant ways.

Across each stage of my training, I have been drawn to the same intersection: where engineering principles become biomedical tools, and where those tools can help move research closer to patient impact.

Research & Experience

August 2023 – Present

Graduate Researcher

Medical Device Development for Osteoarthritis Research | Neu Research Lab, University of Colorado Boulder

Under the mentorship of Dr. Corey Neu, I am developing and characterizing a medical device platform designed to support regenerative therapies and drug discovery for osteoarthritis.

My research focuses on a joint-on-chip system that models communication between cartilage and bone in a controlled microscale environment. The platform is designed to recreate important features of the joint microenvironment, including biological signaling and mechanical loading, to better understand early osteoarthritis disease processes.

I am responsible for advancing the device's mechanical component through design, characterization, validation, and computational modeling. This includes evaluating device performance experimentally and using Abaqus to model mechanical behavior, optimize design decisions, and better understand how the system responds under physiologically relevant loading conditions.

Over the past year, I have advanced the mechanical characterization and validation of the platform to support future translational development for osteoarthritis research, regenerative therapy development, and drug discovery. I have also explored new design strategies beyond the original pneumatic approach, including magnetic PDMS-based concepts for controlled mechanical stimulation.
